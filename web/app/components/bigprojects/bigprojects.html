The Kitchen Makeover

The poor shape of the kitchen upon finding this house, is quite possibly the reason I was able to afford it. It worked... sure, but the 1970’s oak cabinets, vinal floors and a 4” deep sink (seriously... what can you wash in that?) did not make for a plesant cooking expirience. It was the very first thing to be replaced, and my favorite transformation to date.

Out With the Old, In With the New

I don’t think I lasted even a whole day with the sink. It was really the start the whole thing. Long before I signed the final papers and got the keys to the house I had found and purchased the replacement in the clearance section of Ikea, exactly what I was dreaming of a deep, white cast iron sink - and for a quarter of the price!

We decided to keep the major appliances and plumbing in the same place. The layout was really fine, it was the execution that had all the problems. Ikea kitchen’s offer a ton of delightful space utilizing features and they come at a price that even a brand new homeowner can stomach, plus they have a fantastic tool that let’s you design your entire kitchen online, I was able to mock up my ideal kitchen, in my kitchen!

Okay. Let me get real. I COULD NOT have done the rest of this without the relentless help of my father, who graciously took a break from retirement to be my full time contractor. A million thank yous! Taking apart a kitchen is back-breaking work, and it creates quite a mess...

Do you think you like putting together Ikea furniture? It’s a fun puzzle right? Yeah, I thought so too. Well don’t be fooled, a kitchen is a whole different story. For example, if you try to watch Breaking Bad while puting together your Ikea kitchen, you will hate Breaking Bad, and apparently, that says something.
